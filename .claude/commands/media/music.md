## Best Practices

**1. Start with Mood, Not Genre**
Define the emotional impact first, then select genre. The same mood can be expressed through multiple musical styles - choose based on audience and brand fit.

**2. Voice-Over Friendly Music Selection**
For content with narration, choose music with minimal mid-range frequency content. Instruments like piano, strings, and subtle percussion work better than busy guitar or vocal tracks.

**3. Respect Platform Conventions**
Different platforms have different music expectations. LinkedIn favors professional orchestral or acoustic, while TikTok trends toward contemporary pop and electronic sounds.

**4. Build a Music Library**
Create a curated collection of pre-licensed tracks organized by mood and use case. This dramatically speeds up production for recurring content types.

**5. Tempo Matching for Pacing**
Match music tempo to content pacing. Fast cuts need 120+ BPM, while contemplative content works at 60-90 BPM. Mismatch creates cognitive dissonance.

**6. The 30-Second Rule**
Viewers decide to continue watching within 30 seconds. Use your most engaging music in the first half-minute to hook attention and set expectations.

**7. Strategic Silence**
Don't fill every second with music. Strategic silence or minimal ambient sound can create powerful emphasis and give viewers mental breathing room.

**8. Test Across Devices**
Preview music on phone speakers, headphones, and desktop. Bass-heavy tracks may lose impact on mobile devices. Ensure clarity across all playback environments.

**9. Archive Everything**
Save project files, stems, and licensing documentation. Future edits, content repurposing, or legal questions require access to original materials.

**10. Trend Awareness vs. Timelessness**
Balance trending sounds for immediate engagement with timeless elements for content longevity. Overly trendy music dates content quickly.

## Troubleshooting

**Issue: Music Overpowers Dialogue**

- Reduce music volume to -20dB to -30dB during speech
- Apply audio ducking automation to create space
- Choose music with less mid-range frequency content
- Use EQ to reduce frequencies where voice sits (1-4kHz)

**Issue: Track Doesn't Match Video Length**

- Use seamless loop creation to extend naturally
- Edit track structure (repeat chorus, extend bridge)
- Apply time-stretching carefully (max 5% without artifacts)
- Generate custom AI track at exact duration needed

**Issue: Copyright Claims on YouTube**

- Verify license includes YouTube rights
- Upload license documentation to YouTube Studio
- Use Content ID claim resolution system
- Switch to YouTube Audio Library or verified sources

**Issue: Music Feels Generic or Boring**

- Layer multiple tracks for unique sound combinations
- Add subtle sound effects for character
- Use unexpected genre combinations
- Apply creative processing (reverse, half-speed, filtering)

**Issue: Inconsistent Brand Sound**

- Create formal audio brand guidelines document
- Build approved music library for team use
- Implement music approval workflow
- Conduct team training on brand audio standards
