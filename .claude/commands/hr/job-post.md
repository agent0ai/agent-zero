## Implementation Steps

### Step 1: Role Definition & Requirements Gathering

**Objective**: Collect comprehensive job requirements and context

**Actions**:

1. Define job title and level (entry, mid, senior, executive)
2. Identify department and reporting structure
3. Determine location (on-site, hybrid, remote, multi-location)
4. Establish salary range and compensation structure
5. List required qualifications and experience
6. Identify preferred/nice-to-have skills
7. Define key responsibilities (3-6 primary duties)
8. Understand team dynamics and culture fit
9. Clarify growth opportunities and career path
10. Gather hiring manager priorities and deal-breakers

**Validation**: Complete role profile with all essential information

**Information Required**:

- Job title (exact and any alternative titles)
- Department and team structure
- Reporting relationships (manager and direct reports)
- Location and remote work policy
- Salary range (or budget approval level)
- Years of experience required
- Required education and certifications
- Must-have technical skills
- Preferred qualifications
- Success metrics for role (30/60/90 days)

### Step 2: Competitive Research & Market Analysis

**Objective**: Understand market positioning and competitive landscape

**Actions**:

1. Search similar roles on major job boards (LinkedIn, Indeed, Glassdoor)
2. Analyze competitor job postings in industry
3. Research salary benchmarks for role and location
4. Review benefits and perks being offered by competitors
5. Identify trending keywords in high-performing postings
6. Analyze job title variations and effectiveness
7. Study application requirements (resume, portfolio, tests)
8. Review employer brand messaging of top companies
9. Identify gaps in competitor offerings
10. Determine unique selling propositions

**Validation**: Comprehensive competitive intelligence gathered

**Research Areas**:

- Competitor job titles for same role
- Salary ranges by location and experience level
- Common benefits packages in industry
- Application conversion rates (if available)
- Time-to-fill benchmarks
- Candidate preference trends

### Step 3: Job Title Optimization

**Objective**: Create SEO-optimized, clear, and attractive job title

**Actions**:

1. Use industry-standard terminology (avoid internal jargon)
2. Include seniority level in title (Junior, Senior, Lead, etc.)
3. Incorporate primary keyword for searchability
4. Keep title concise (under 60 characters)
5. Test variations for search volume
6. Ensure ATS compatibility (standard formatting)
7. Verify clarity for external candidates
8. Check against job board best practices
9. Avoid superlatives and marketing language
10. Confirm alignment with career progression

**Validation**: Clear, searchable job title optimized for discovery

**Title Best Practices**:

- Format: [Level] [Role] - [Specialization] (if needed)
- Examples: "Senior Software Engineer - Backend", "Marketing Manager", "Director of Sales Operations"
- Avoid: "Marketing Ninja", "Code Wizard", "Sales Rockstar"
- Include remote/hybrid if applicable: "Remote Customer Success Manager"

### Step 4: Compelling Summary Creation

**Objective**: Write engaging opening that sells the opportunity

**Actions**:

1. Lead with company mission and impact
2. Describe the role's importance to organization
3. Highlight key responsibilities in 2-3 sentences
4. Emphasize growth and learning opportunities
5. Include unique aspects of the position
6. Mention team size and structure
7. Note any exciting projects or initiatives
8. Keep summary to 3-5 sentences (75-100 words)
9. Use active, engaging language
10. Include company value proposition

**Validation**: Summary excites candidates and clearly communicates opportunity

**Summary Formula**:
[Company context] is seeking a [Job Title] to [primary responsibility]. In this role, you'll [key impact], working with [team/technologies]. This is an opportunity to [growth/learning benefit] while [company mission contribution].

Example: "TechCorp, a rapidly growing SaaS platform serving 10,000+ customers, is seeking a Senior Product Manager to lead our AI-powered analytics suite. In this role, you'll define product strategy, collaborate with engineering and design teams, and drive features that process billions of data points daily. This is an opportunity to shape the future of business intelligence while working with cutting-edge AI/ML technologies."

### Step 5: Responsibilities & Impact Section

**Objective**: Define clear, action-oriented responsibilities

**Actions**:

1. List 5-8 primary responsibilities
2. Lead each bullet with strong action verb
3. Include scope and scale (team size, budget, metrics)
4. Emphasize impact and outcomes over tasks
5. Organize by priority or frequency
6. Balance strategic and tactical responsibilities
7. Include collaboration and stakeholder management
8. Specify tools and technologies used
9. Mention autonomy and decision-making authority
10. Highlight opportunities for innovation

**Validation**: Responsibilities clearly communicate day-to-day work and impact

**Responsibility Formula**:
[Action Verb] + [What] + [How/With Whom] + [Expected Outcome/Impact]

**Strong Action Verbs by Function**:

- Strategy: Define, Develop, Drive, Lead, Shape
- Execution: Build, Implement, Launch, Deliver, Execute
- Collaboration: Partner, Coordinate, Align, Facilitate
- Analysis: Analyze, Assess, Monitor, Optimize, Track
- Communication: Present, Communicate, Articulate, Influence

### Step 6: Qualifications & Requirements

**Objective**: Clearly define required and preferred qualifications

**Actions**:

1. Separate "Required" from "Preferred" qualifications
2. List education requirements (degree type, field)
3. Specify years of experience needed
4. Detail technical skills and proficiency levels
5. Include industry-specific certifications
6. Mention domain expertise required
7. List soft skills critical to role success
8. Specify tools and platforms experience
9. Note language requirements if applicable
10. Avoid unnecessary barriers (remove "nice-to-haves" from required)

**Validation**: Qualifications are inclusive, realistic, and role-appropriate

**Qualification Structure**:

**Required Qualifications**:

- [X] years of experience in [field/role]
- [Specific technical skills or tools]
- [Education requirement] in [field]
- [Critical soft skills]
- [Industry-specific requirements]

**Preferred Qualifications**:

- [Nice-to-have experience]
- [Additional certifications]
- [Bonus technical skills]
- [Previous industry experience]

**Inclusivity Check**:

- Remove gendered language
- Avoid age-related requirements ("recent graduate", "digital native")
- Question necessity of degree requirements
- Limit years of experience to true minimum
- Use "preferred" generously to expand candidate pool

## Best Practices

**1. Transparency Builds Trust**
Including salary ranges, even when not legally required, increases application rates by 30%+ and attracts more qualified candidates who know they're in range.

**2. Show, Don't Just Tell**
Instead of "fast-paced environment," describe actual pace: "Launch new features bi-weekly," "Daily standups and weekly sprint planning."

**3. Keep It Scannable**
Candidates spend 14 seconds on initial scan. Use bullet points, headers, and short paragraphs. Wall of text = immediate bounce.

**4. Sell the Mission, Not Just the Job**
Top candidates want impact. Lead with why the role matters, not just what the tasks are. Connect daily work to company mission.

**5. Remove Unnecessary Barriers**
Every requirement reduces applicant pool by ~10%. Women apply when they meet 100% of qualifications, men at 60%. Move nice-to-haves to "preferred."

**6. Update Regularly**
Job postings older than 30 days get 50% fewer applications. Re-post or refresh posting date to maintain visibility on job boards.

**7. Use Real Titles**
"Marketing Ninja" might be fun internally, but candidates search for "Marketing Manager." Use standard titles for discovery, add personality in description.

**8. Highlight Remote Work Clearly**
"Remote" in title increases applications by 300%. Be specific: remote-friendly vs remote-first vs distributed team. Location restrictions matter.

**9. Benefits Matter as Much as Salary**
40% of candidates prioritize benefits over salary. Don't bury them at the end—highlight unique benefits early.

**10. Test and Iterate**
A/B test job titles, summary language, and benefits emphasis. Track which postings generate highest-quality applicants, not just volume.

## Troubleshooting

**Issue: Low Application Volume**

- Expand salary range or increase transparency
- Broaden qualifications (move to "preferred")
- Improve job title SEO
- Boost posting on job boards
- Share more widely on social media
- Add remote work flexibility if possible

**Issue: Many Unqualified Applicants**

- Clarify required qualifications vs preferred
- Add pre-screening questions
- Make responsibilities more specific
- Include technical requirements early
- Add assessment or portfolio requirement
- Improve job title specificity

**Issue: Candidates Dropping Off During Application**

- Simplify application process (reduce required fields)
- Remove unnecessary assessments upfront
- Make mobile application friendly
- Provide estimated completion time
- Save progress functionality
- Clear instructions and expectations

**Issue: Low Diversity in Applicant Pool**

- Audit language for unconscious bias
- Remove unnecessary degree requirements
- Add inclusive benefits (parental leave, etc.)
- Post on diversity-focused job boards
- Partner with diversity organizations
- Highlight diversity initiatives in posting

**Issue: Posting Not Ranking on Job Boards**

- Increase keyword density
- Update posting to refresh date
- Use sponsored/featured placement
- Improve title SEO
- Add more detail (aim for 800+ words)
- Include all structured data fields
